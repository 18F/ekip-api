Plan Your Trip

"In every walk with nature, one receives far more than he seeks." - John Muir

Your paper voucher opens the door to new adventures and discovery of the great outdoors. Our federal lands and waters provide an amazing and fulfilling experience with a little bit of planning.

Explore Various Destinations

These cool trip planning tools (from our partner agencies), can help you find a destination. There are many federal lands and waters you can visit, and each presents a whole variety of activities you can do.

  • National Wildlife Refuge System: national wildlife refuges
  • Discover the Forest:: national forests and other areas
  • Find Your Park: national parks

Lodging

Explore lodging options before you start out. Where you are looking for hotels, campgrouns or back country camping, we recommend reserving a spot when possible. See recreation.gov to make online reservations.

Packing List

These are some essentials you should consider carrying with you.
  • Plenty of water
  • Snacks (high energy snacks such as trail mix and protein bars are great)
  • A small first-aid kit
  • Sunglasses, hat, and sunscreen (or gloves and a warm hat depending on the season)
  • Bug repellent
  • A map of the area and emergency numbers

Exchange Your Voucher

At the following sites you can exchange your paper voucher for a wallet-sized pass. All of these sites are amazing places to visit.

View Pass Sites

You might not have cell phone coverage. For example, Glacier National Park is located in the mountains which are so big and remote you can't make or receive phone calls, texts or emails. Not all sites are like this, but it's best not to rely on your cell phone when you visit.

Don't forget! You'll also need to bring the printed paper voucher instead of an electronic copy on your phone or tablet. The sites do not have the ability to accept electronic vouchers. Also, in some cases, you may need to leave the paper copy on the dashboard of your vehicle.
