Every Kid in a Park - Statistics

Paper Passes

Numbers for Paper Passes represent the number of times the Every Kid in a Park website has generated an electronic paper pass for a website visitor. Website visitors include 4th grade students, parents and educators, but they also include other members of the general public and other visitors.

The numbers reported here represent aggregate counts of the number of times an electronic paper pass have been generated. They do not represent:

  • The number of 4th graders who have generated an electronic paper pass.
  • The number of times an electronic paper pass has been printed.
  • The number of 4th graders reached through this program
  • For the educator workflow, the number of educators who have used the system.

    • If you're still not sure what these numbers represent or if you're interpreting them correctly, please ask us!

    The system has generated {{num_tickets_issued|intcomma}} paper passes through the student workflow since September 1st, 2015.

    Of which, {{educator_tickets_issued|intcomma}} paper passes have been generated through the educator workflow.

    Exchanges

    Exchanges represent the physical exchange of the printed, paper pass for the plastic pass. These represent numbers reported directly by the fee collectors.

    These numbers represent the aggregate count of paper passes that have been reported exchanged for plastic passes by fee collectors. These numbers do not represent:

  • The number of paper pass holders who have used the paper pass to gain entry to a federal land or water.
  • The number of 4th graders who have received a plastic pass.
  • The number of 4th graders who have exchanged a paper pass for a plastic pass (this number does not include numbers reported by Army Corps of Engineers, and it includes anyone who was able to succesfully exchange the paper pass for a plastic one).

    • {{num_tickets_exchanged|intcomma}} paper passes have been reported as exchanged for plastic passes.

    {{all_exchanged|intcomma}} paper passes have been reported as exchanged for plastic passes (including those that have been redeemed more than once).
